5 Common Fire Hazards in Modern Office Buildings.

Modern offices are designed for collaboration and productivity, but they also introduce unique fire safety challenges that are often overlooked. As technology advances, our workplaces evolve, bringing new risks with them.

1. Overloaded Electrical Power Strips

With dozens of monitors, computers, and peripheral devices, electrical outlets are under more strain than ever. Daisychaining power strips—plugging one into another—is a leading cause of electrical fires in commercial buildings.

2. Blocked Emergency Exits

It sounds simple, but temporary storage of deliveries or office furniture in corridors can be fatal during an evacuation. Every second counts when a fire starts, and a clear path to safety is non-negotiable.

"Fire safety isn't just about having the right equipment; it's about maintaining a culture of awareness at every level of the organization."

3. Improper Storage of Flammables

Even in paperless offices, cleaning supplies and certain tech maintenance chemicals are often stored incorrectly. Proper ventilation and designated storage areas are essential for these materials.
